Introduction

Biomolecules are organic compounds present in living organisms that perform essential biological functions such as energy supply, growth, metabolism, and genetic control. Major classes include carbohydrates, proteins, vitamins, enzymes, hormones, and nucleic acids.

Key Definitions

  • Carbohydrates: Polyhydroxy aldehydes or ketones or compounds that yield them on hydrolysis.
  • Monosaccharides: Simplest carbohydrates that cannot be hydrolysed.
  • Glycosidic Bond: Linkage between two monosaccharide units.
  • Reducing Sugar: Sugar capable of reducing Tollens' or Fehling's reagent.
  • Amino Acids: Compounds containing –NH₂ and –COOH groups.
  • Zwitter Ion: Dipolar form (NH₃⁺–CHR–COO⁻).
  • Peptide Bond: –CO–NH– linkage between amino acids.
  • Denaturation: Loss of biological activity due to structural change.
  • Nucleotide: Base + Sugar + Phosphate.
  • Nucleoside: Base + Sugar.

Main Concepts

1. Carbohydrates

Classification

  • Monosaccharides
  • Oligosaccharides (2–10 units)
  • Polysaccharides

Monosaccharides

  • General formula: Cₙ(H₂O)ₙ
  • Aldoses and Ketoses
  • Show optical isomerism

D and L Configuration

  • Based on –OH on last chiral carbon
  • D-form → right
  • L-form → left

Glucose

  • Open chain + cyclic form
  • Cyclic via hemiacetal formation
  • α and β forms
  • Shows mutarotation

Important Reactions of Glucose

  • Oxidation → Gluconic acid
  • Reduction → Sorbitol
  • Acetylation → Glucose pentaacetate
  • With NH₂OH → Oxime
  • With HCN → Cyanohydrin

Glycosidic Linkage

  • α-1,4 in maltose
  • α-1,2 in sucrose

Reducing vs Non-Reducing

  • Reducing: Glucose, Maltose, Lactose
  • Non-reducing: Sucrose

Polysaccharides

  • Starch → storage (amylose + amylopectin)
  • Cellulose → structural
  • Glycogen → animal storage

2. Proteins

Amino Acids

  • General: NH₂–CHR–COOH
  • Zwitter ion in solution
  • Essential & Non-essential

Isoelectric Point

pH at which net charge is zero

Peptide Bond

Condensation → –CO–NH–

Structure of Proteins

  • Primary → sequence
  • Secondary → α-helix, β-sheet
  • Tertiary → folding
  • Quaternary → multiple chains

Stabilizing Forces

  • Hydrogen bonds
  • Disulfide bonds
  • Van der Waals

Types

  • Fibrous → structural
  • Globular → functional

Denaturation

  • Secondary & tertiary lost
  • Primary intact

3. Enzymes

  • Biological catalysts
  • Lock and Key model
  • Affected by temperature, pH, substrate

4. Hormones

  • Chemical messengers
  • Examples: Insulin, Thyroxine

5. Vitamins

  • Fat soluble: A, D, E, K
  • Water soluble: B, C

Deficiency Diseases

  • A → Night blindness
  • B₁ → Beriberi
  • C → Scurvy
  • D → Rickets

6. Nucleic Acids

Structure

  • Nucleotide = Base + Sugar + Phosphate
  • DNA → double helix
  • RNA → single strand

Base Pairing

  • A = T (2 H-bonds)
  • G ≡ C (3 H-bonds)

DNA vs RNA

  • DNA → Deoxyribose, Thymine
  • RNA → Ribose, Uracil

Important Formulas

Carbohydrate: Cₙ(H₂O)ₙ

Zwitter Ion: NH₃⁺–CHR–COO⁻

Peptide Formation:

Amino Acid + Amino Acid → Dipeptide + H₂O

Important Points

  • Sucrose is non-reducing
  • Glucose shows mutarotation
  • Denaturation does NOT break peptide bonds
  • Enzymes are specific
  • DNA shows complementary pairing
  • Isoelectric point is important

Common Mistakes

  • D/L ≠ optical rotation
  • Wrong glycosidic bonds
  • DNA vs RNA confusion
  • Denaturation misunderstanding
  • Vitamin diseases forgotten

Quick Revision

  • Carbohydrates → energy
  • Proteins → structure & function
  • Enzymes → catalysts
  • Vitamins → regulators
  • DNA → genetic material
  • RNA → protein synthesis
