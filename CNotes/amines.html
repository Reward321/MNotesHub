📘 Chapter 9: Amines

1. Introduction

Amines are organic derivatives of ammonia (NH₃) formed by replacing hydrogen atoms with alkyl or aryl groups.

  • NH₃ → Ammonia
  • R–NH₂ → Primary (1°)
  • R₂NH → Secondary (2°)
  • R₃N → Tertiary (3°)

Importance: Found in biomolecules, drugs, dyes, polymers.

2. Key Definitions

  • Primary amine: R–NH₂
  • Secondary amine: R₂NH
  • Tertiary amine: R₃N
  • Quaternary salt: R₄N⁺X⁻
  • Aliphatic: Alkyl group
  • Aromatic: Aryl group

3. Classification

  • 1°, 2°, 3° amines
  • Aliphatic / Aromatic
  • Mixed amines

4. Structure

  • Hybridisation: sp³
  • Shape: Trigonal pyramidal
  • Bond angle: ~108°
  • Presence of lone pair

5. Nomenclature

Common Name

Alkyl + amine → CH₃NH₂ = Methylamine

IUPAC Name

Methane → Methanamine

Secondary/Tertiary

Use N- prefix → N-methylmethanamine

6. Preparation

(A) Reduction of Nitro Compounds

R–NO₂ + 3H₂ → R–NH₂ + 2H₂O

(B) Ammonolysis

R–X + NH₃ → R–NH₂

(C) Reduction of Nitriles

R–CN → R–CH₂NH₂

(D) Gabriel Synthesis

Forms only primary amines

(E) Hoffmann Bromamide

R–CONH₂ → R–NH₂ (one carbon less)

7. Physical Properties

  • Lower: gases
  • Higher: liquids/solids
  • Boiling: 1° > 2° > 3°
  • Solubility decreases with chain length
  • Fishy smell

8. Chemical Properties

(A) Basic Nature

R–NH₂ + H₂O ⇌ R–NH₃⁺ + OH⁻

Order:

  • Gas: 3° > 2° > 1°
  • Aqueous: 2° > 1° > 3°

(B) Alkylation

R–NH₂ → R₂NH → R₃N → R₄N⁺

(C) Acylation

R–NH₂ + RCOCl → Amide

(D) Carbylamine Test

R–NH₂ + CHCl₃ + KOH → R–NC (foul smell)

(E) Hinsberg Test

TypeResult
1°Soluble
2°Insoluble
3°No reaction

(F) Nitrous Acid Reaction

  • 1° Aliphatic → Alcohol + N₂
  • 1° Aromatic → Diazonium salt
  • 2° → Nitrosamine
  • 3° → No reaction

9. Reactions of Aniline

  • Bromination: 2,4,6-tribromoaniline
  • Nitration: Mixture
  • Sulphonation: Sulphanilic acid
  • Acetylation: Acetanilide

10. Diazonium Salts

Preparation

Ar–NH₂ + NaNO₂ + HCl → Ar–N₂⁺Cl⁻ (273–278 K)

Reactions

  • Sandmeyer → Ar–Cl/Br
  • Gattermann → Ar–Cl
  • Iodination → Ar–I
  • Schiemann → Ar–F
  • Hydrolysis → Ar–OH
  • Coupling → Azo dyes

11. Uses

  • Dyes
  • Medicines
  • Polymers
  • Rubber
  • Explosives

12. Important Exam Points

  • Aromatic amines less basic
  • Hoffmann → one carbon less
  • Carbylamine → only 1°
  • Diazonium salts need low temperature

13. Common Mistakes

  • Wrong basicity order
  • Ignoring temperature
  • Wrong Hoffmann product
  • Misusing tests

14. Quick Revision

  • NH₃ derivatives
  • sp³ structure
  • Preparation: Reduction, Gabriel, Hoffmann
  • Tests: Carbylamine, Hinsberg

15. MCQs

Q1. Most basic in aqueous solution?
A. 1° B. 2° C. 3° D. NH₃
Answer: B

Q2. Hoffmann reaction gives:
A. Same B. +1 C. –1 D. No change
Answer: C

Q3. Carbylamine test is for:
A. 1° B. 2° C. 3° D. All
Answer: A

Q4. Stability temperature:
A. 300K B. 273–278K C. 350K D. 0K
Answer: B

Q5. Aniline less basic due to:
A. +I B. Resonance C. Induction D. Hybridisation
Answer: B
